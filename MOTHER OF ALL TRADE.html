After nearly 20 years of complex negotiations, India and the European Union have signed a landmark Free Trade Agreement (FTA). Prime Minister Narendra Modi hails it as a transformative pact that will lift manufacturing, expand services, and cement a strategic technology and defense partnership.

Global Economic Weight

This agreement is not just bilateral; it is a global economic shifter. The combined entity of India and the EU represents a massive portion of the world's economy.

25% of Global GDP

1/3rd of Global Trade

Shattering Trade Barriers

One of the most aggressive aspects of the deal is the near-total elimination of tariffs. This move opens the floodgates for Indian exports to European markets.

Key Insight: 96% of trade will gradually become tariff-free, significantly reducing costs for exporters and consumers alike.

Top Beneficiary Sectors

Specific industries are poised for immediate growth due to duty-free access. Marine products, textiles, and leather goods stand to gain the most.

*Relative impact potential based on duty-free access priority.

A Long Road to Victory

Trade deals are notoriously difficult. This agreement was nearly two decades in the making, yet it concluded faster than the EU-Mercosur negotiation.

2026: The Year of Diversification

The deal comes at a critical geopolitical juncture. As US trade policy becomes unpredictable with threats of tariff hikes (up to 75%), India is strategically pivoting to diversify its partnerships.

The US Risk

Tariffs on Indian imports are currently high (50%) and could rise to 75% under new threats.

The EU Solution

The FTA provides a stable, low-tariff alternative, reducing economic dependence on Washington.
